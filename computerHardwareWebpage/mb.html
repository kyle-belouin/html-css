MOTHERBOARDS

PURPOSE AND FUNCTION

Overview

  • Motherboards provide the physical mounting point of all hardware needed for a computer to function as well as accessory components.
  • The motherboard facilitates the circuitry needed for all the individual components to work together.
  • The motherboard is the heart of the overall computer system. All physical input and output relative to user experience lives on the motherboard.
  • Motherboards of various sizes and capacity exist for various different applications.
  • The motherboard is mounted on the case or box of the computer on stand-offs so it does not touch the case directly. This allows the motherboard to be electrically grounded.

Motherboard Sizes and Typical Uses

- Mini-ITX -
170mm x 170mm | Very small form factor PCs | Media Servers | Ultra-Portable, low-power gaming PCs

- Micro-ATX -
240mm x 240mm | Medium-sized form factor | General use PCs | Low to Mid-Range gaming capabilities

- ATX -
305 x 244 mm | Full-sized form factor | High performance PCs | Mid-Range to High-end gaming capabilities | Strong Virtualization capabilities

- Extended-ATX -
305mm x 330 mm | Large form factor | Highest performance consumer PCs | High-end gaming and multimedia processing capabilities | Consumer Servers/Workstations | Generally capable of having multiple processors.
